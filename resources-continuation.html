â—ˆ GLOSSARY OF BLACK HAT TERMS
Term Definition
Black Hat SEO Optimization techniques that violate search engine guidelines to manipulate rankings
Cloaking Showing different content to search engines than to users, often to hide spam or manipulative content
PBN Private Blog Network - collection of sites built specifically to provide backlinks to money sites
Parasite SEO Publishing content on high-authority platforms (Medium, LinkedIn) to rank faster by leveraging their domain authority
CTR Manipulation Artificially inflating click-through rates in search results using bots or paid clickers
Link Farm Group of websites that all link to every other site in the group to artificially boost link metrics
Doorway Pages Pages created solely to rank for specific keywords and funnel traffic to another destination
Keyword Stuffing Overloading content with target keywords in unnatural way to manipulate relevance signals
Hidden Text/Links Content invisible to users (white text on white background, CSS hidden) but visible to search engines
Negative SEO Using black hat tactics against competitors' sites to damage their rankings
Link Injection Hacking websites to inject backlinks to target sites, also called "hacklinks"
Content Scraping Automatically copying content from other sites and republishing it (with or without modification)
Article Spinning Using software to rewrite existing articles by replacing words with synonyms to avoid duplicate content detection
GMB/GBP Google My Business / Google Business Profile - local listing system frequently targeted by black hat tactics
E-E-A-T Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness - Google quality signals black hats attempt to fake
Schema Markup Abuse Manipulating structured data to display misleading information in search results
Domain Squatting Registering domains similar to popular brands to capture their traffic
Expired Domains Previously registered domains with existing authority that are repurposed for black hat campaigns
SERP Volatility Fluctuations in search rankings, often caused by algorithm updates or black hat detection
De-indexing Removal of pages/sites from search engine index, often as penalty for black hat tactics
